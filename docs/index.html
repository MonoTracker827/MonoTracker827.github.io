MonoTracker

Monocular RGB-Only 6D Tracking of Unknown Objects

BMVC 2025 (Oral)

Zilong Deng1,2 Shaochang Tan1,2 Zuria Bauer2 Daniel Barath2 Marc Pollefeys2,3
1University of Zurich 2ETH Zurich 3Microsoft
Paper Code

MonoTracker takes an image sequence as input, utilizes monocular depth prediction, and estimates the 6D object trajectory. It achieves significantly more accurate results than the recent BundleSDF on several public datasets.

Abstract

Estimating the six degrees of freedom (6D) pose of unknown objects using only monocular RGB images is a challenging task, especially when dealing with textureless and small objects. % partially occluded, In this paper, we propose a novel pipeline, MonoTracker, for 6D object pose estimation and tracking that operates without any prior depth information. MonoTracker is a model-free, RGB-only, 6D detector that works on unseen objects. It leverages state-of-the-art pre-trained deep learning models, enabling zero-shot 6D pose estimation by jointly optimizing object poses and correcting scale inconsistencies in monocular depth predictions. We validate our method on three public datasets -- YCBInEOAT, HO3D, and BEHAVE -- demonstrating significant improvements over the state of the art. As a downstream application, we also show that the estimated camera poses can be used as input in NeRF pipelines, facilitating novel-view synthesis. Our results highlight the potential of monocular RGB inputs for accurate 6D object tracking and reconstruction in real-world scenarios.
